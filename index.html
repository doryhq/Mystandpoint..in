Featured Observation

Kumbh Mela, Prayagraj, India

The Internet Didn't Replace Ritual

For years, technology promised to make physical gatherings less important. Yet hundreds of millions still travelled across India for Kumbh Mela. The value was never information. The value was participation. Technology scales access. Ritual scales belonging.

The Standpoint: People don't gather for information. They gather for the feeling that information alone cannot deliver.

What We Believe
The strongest brands are not always the most visible. They are often the most clearly understood.

Every interaction, message and impression builds a standpoint in the mind of the consumer. Understanding that standpoint is often more valuable than simply increasing visibility.

Questions We're Often Asked

What is brand perception?

Brand perception is how your audience actually experiences your brand — what they feel, remember and associate with you — which often differs from what the business intends to communicate.

Why does consumer perception matter for brand strategy?

Because people form opinions before they make decisions. The brand that exists in someone else's mind is the one that drives preference, trust and choice.

What is brand positioning?

Brand positioning is the process of defining a clear, meaningful place in the minds of your target audience — distinct from competitors and relevant to the people you serve.

Short observations on brands, culture and human behaviour. From boardrooms and bazaars. From Tokyo to Tiruppur.

The Luxury of Being Difficult to Access

Global — Hermès, Rolex, Nobu

Most brands try to remove friction. Luxury brands often create it. Getting a bag from Hermès or a table at Nobu can take longer than people expect. Yet the waiting becomes part of the value. Sometimes people don't desire what is expensive. They desire what feels difficult to obtain.

The Standpoint: Scarcity is not just a supply decision. It is a perception strategy.

Why Fevicol Became Bigger Than Glue

India

Nobody talks about adhesive strength when they talk about Fevicol. They talk about overcrowded buses, impossible bonds, furniture that never breaks. The strongest brands stop describing products and start describing life. People rarely remember product benefits. They remember stories.

The Standpoint: The brand that lives in culture lives longer than the brand that lives in advertising.

Why Apple's Launches Feel Like Festivals

Cupertino, California

Apple does not launch products. It holds events. The language is ceremonial. The venue is theatrical. The audience queues like pilgrims. When a brand understands that people need rituals — not just reasons — it stops competing on features and starts competing on feeling.

The Standpoint: The most loyal customers aren't convinced. They are converted.

Why Google Glass Failed

Mountain View, California

The technology worked. The social behaviour didn't. People were uncomfortable being recorded by someone who looked like they were wearing a camera. Many products fail because they solve a technical problem while creating a social one.

The Standpoint: Products are adopted by individuals but experienced in public. Both contexts must work.

What Zara Understood About Desire

A Coruña, Spain

Zara doesn't have sales. It has scarcity. When a garment is gone, it's gone. Customers visit more frequently not because of loyalty — but because of anxiety. The fear of missing something creates behaviour that no loyalty programme could manufacture.

The Standpoint: Urgency is more powerful than incentive. Fear of loss moves faster than promise of gain.

Brand Perception: A Reference

Clear definitions for the concepts that shape how brands are built, remembered and chosen. Written to be understood, not just cited.

What is brand perception?

Brand perception is the sum of everything an audience feels, remembers and associates with a brand — built through every interaction, message and experience, often independent of what the brand intends. It lives in the minds of people, not in brand guidelines.

What is brand positioning?

Brand positioning is the deliberate process of defining and communicating a clear, meaningful and differentiated place in the minds of a target audience. A well-positioned brand is not just recognised — it is preferred for a reason.

What is the perception-reality gap?

The perception-reality gap is the distance between what a brand believes it communicates and what its audience actually understands. This gap is where trust is eroded, preference is lost and opportunities are missed.

What is consumer trust?

Consumer trust is the confidence an audience holds in a brand's consistency, honesty and intent. It is not declared — it is earned through repeated experience. Trust is fragile: slow to build, fast to break and difficult to rebuild.

What is brand strategy?

Brand strategy is the long-term plan for how a brand will be perceived, positioned and communicated to create preference and relevance. It connects business objectives with audience understanding.

What is a brand standpoint?

A brand standpoint is the position a brand occupies in the mind of a consumer — shaped by direct experiences, stories heard and associations formed. It is the most honest measure of a brand's true strength.
