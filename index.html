Featured Observation

Kumbh Mela, Prayagraj, India

The Internet Did Not Replace Ritual

For years, technology promised to make physical gatherings less important. Yet hundreds of millions still travelled across India to participate in Kumbh Mela. Everyone already knew the stories. Everyone already knew the significance. The value was never information. The value was participation. Technology scales access. Ritual scales belonging.

The StandpointTechnology can scale access, but only ritual can scale belonging.

The Luxury of Being Difficult to Access

Global, Hermes, Rolex, Nobu

Most brands try to remove friction. Luxury brands often create it. Getting a bag from Hermes or a table at Nobu can take longer than people expect. Yet the waiting becomes part of the value. Sometimes people do not desire what is expensive. They desire what feels difficult to obtain.

The StandpointScarcity is not just a supply decision. It is a perception strategy.

Why Fevicol Became Bigger Than Glue

India

Nobody talks about adhesive strength when they talk about Fevicol. They talk about overcrowded buses, impossible bonds, furniture that never breaks. The strongest brands stop describing products and start describing life. People rarely remember product benefits. They remember stories.

The StandpointThe brand that lives in culture lives longer than the brand that lives in advertising.

What Zara Understood About Desire

A Coruna, Spain

Zara does not have sales. It has scarcity. When a garment is gone, it is gone. Customers visit more frequently not because of loyalty, but because of anxiety. The fear of missing something creates behaviour that no loyalty programme could manufacture.

The StandpointUrgency is more powerful than incentive. Fear of loss moves faster than promise of gain.

Why Apple Launches Feel Like Festivals

Cupertino, California

Apple does not launch products. It holds events. The language is ceremonial. The venue is theatrical. The audience queues like pilgrims. When a brand understands that people need rituals, not just reasons, it stops competing on features and starts competing on feeling.

The StandpointThe most loyal customers are not convinced. They are converted.

The Campaign Was About AI. The Insight Was About Access.

India, Cadbury and Shah Rukh Khan

Cadbury allowed local shopkeepers to generate personalised ads featuring Shah Rukh Khan. The technology attracted attention. The insight created impact. People have always wanted celebrity endorsements. What changed was access. Technology becomes powerful when it transforms something exclusive into something personal.

The StandpointTechnology earns attention. Access earns affection.

Why Airport Luxury Stores Keep Winning

Global Airports

Most people do not walk into an airport intending to buy a luxury watch. Yet luxury brands continue to occupy some of the most expensive real estate in the world. The store is not only selling products. It is selling possibility. Aspiration is strongest when people are in transit between where they are and where they want to be.

The StandpointAspiration sells hardest in the moments between who we are and who we hope to become.

Why Google Glass Failed

Mountain View, California

The technology worked. The social behaviour did not. People were uncomfortable being recorded by someone who looked like they were wearing a camera. Many products fail because they solve a technical problem while creating a social one.

The StandpointA product is only as successful as the social permission it earns.

Questions

Questions we are often asked.

What is brand perception?

Brand perception is how your audience actually experiences your brand. What they feel, remember and associate with you often differs from what the business intends to communicate.

Why does consumer perception matter for brand strategy?

People form opinions before they make decisions. The brand that exists in someone else's mind is the one that drives preference, trust and choice.

What is brand positioning?

Brand positioning is the process of defining a clear, meaningful place in the minds of your target audience, distinct from competitors and relevant to the people you serve.
